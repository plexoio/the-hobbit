The Hobbit" is a fantasy novel by J.R.R. Tolkien that tells the story of Bilbo Baggins, a hobbit who joins a group of dwarves on a quest to reclaim their homeland from a dragon. Throughout their journey, they encounter various challenges and adversaries while Bilbo discovers courage and resourcefulness within himself. The novel is set in the enchanting world of Middle-earth and serves as a prequel to Tolkien's epic trilogy, "The Lord of the Rings.

Bilbo Baggins

The titular character, a hobbit who is recruited by Gandalf and the dwarves to join their quest. He undergoes personal growth and transforms into a brave and resourceful adventurer.

Gandalf the Grey

A wise and powerful wizard who orchestrates the adventure and provides guidance to Bilbo and the dwarves. He plays a significant role in the events of both "The Hobbit" and "The Lord of the Rings."
